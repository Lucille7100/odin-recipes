Sinigang

Description

Among one the of OG Filipino dishes. To fully experienced Philippines, eating Adobo is a must! Have a taste of slowly cooked meat in it's marinade of soy sauce, garlic, red onions and vinegar.

Ingredients

  • A mix of Pork belly/Pork bone/Spare ribs
  • 2 pieces, rougly chopped- Radish
  • 2 pieces, rougly chopped- Eggplant
  • 2 pieces,sliced- Tomato
  • A handful of Ladies Fingers
  • 2 pieces, rougly chopped- Onion
  • Fish sauce and ground black to taste
  • 3 tablespoons- cooking oil
  • 2 packs- Knorr Tamarind Soup Mix
  • Salt
  • Bunchful of Kangkong (spinach for alternative)
  • 2 pieces, roughly chopped- Taro
  • Fish Sauce
  • Chopped- String Beans
  • Handful of Serrano Chilli

Steps

  1. Grab your mom's BEST COOKING POT, again. Put all meat and fill with water.
  2. Turn on the stove and boil the meat for 30 minutes.
  3. After 30 minutes, throw in your tomatoes and taro. Boil again for another half hour, lid on.
  4. After half an hour, crushed the tomatoes using your spatula to give more flavor. Guess using our last ingredient, patience, would be the best course of action at this moment.
  5. Time to use our secret weapon, Knorr Tamarind Mix. Pour the two packs of mix into boiling soup.
  6. Add 1 teaspoon of Salt
  7. Thorougly mix
  8. Add radish, eggplant and string beans into the pot. At this point, DO NOT STIR. Let the vegetables sit on top and continue to boil for 10 minutes with lid on.
  9. Add ladies fingers, serrano chilli, kangkong or spinach and slowly mix everything.
  10. Continue to boil till the newly added vegatables are half-cooked.
  11. Stir, taste and add 1 teaspoon of fish sauce.
  12. Time to turn off the stove.

TIP FROM THE ANCIENT

You can use any meat for this dish, be it beef or fish!
