Adobo




Description

Among one the of OG Filipino dishes. To fully experienced Philippines, eating Adobo is a must! Have a taste of slowly cooked meat in it's marinade of soy sauce, garlic, red onions and vinegar. ADOBO FOR THE GODS!

Ingredients

  • 1 kilo- Pork belly/ Chicken thigh
  • 1/2 cup- Soy sauce
  • 1 1/2 cup- Vinegar
  • 2 pieces, chopped- Garlic
  • 2 pieces, chopped - Red onions
  • 2 teaspoons- Ground pepper
  • 1 teaspoon- Peppercorn
  • 4-6 leaves- Bay leaf
  • 2 tablespoons- Knorr chicken powder (Classic Filipino secret weapon)
  • 1/2 cup- Sugar
  • P A T I E N C E

Steps

  1. Grab your mom's treasured food bowl.
  2. Throw the following in your bowl:
    • Pork belly/ Chicken thigh
    • Red onions
    • Garlic
    • Peppercorn
    • Bay leaf
    • Peppercorn
    • Vinegar
    • Soy sauce
    • Sugar
    • Knorr powder
  3. MIX ALL, put your heart into it!
  4. Transfer the concotion to your mom's BEST COOKING POT.
  5. Turn on the stove, set the roof on fire!
  6. Place your pot on the stove and set it on medium heat for 10 minutes.
  7. Then set heat from medium to low for 15 minutes with lid on.
  8. Stir occasionally
  9. After approximately 15 minutes, check, stir, and taste. See if the balance suits your palate.
  10. Slowly cook it under low heat for another 30 minutes.
  11. S T I R occasionally or suffer your mom's wrath for burning the bottom of her pot. About time to use our last ingredient, patience.
  12. I know, it smells great already. After 30 minutes, stir for the nth time and witness the fruits of your effort.
  13. Finally the most awaited time has come, to turn off the stove!

TIPS FROM THE ANCIENT

  • No salt, soy sauce does wonders.
  • Marinating the mixed ingredients overnight will be your best decision ever!
  • Paired with rice will be your BEST BEST BEST decision prior to the one above.
  • No water! I know, you must be kiddin me!(For the brave: ADD WATER, it's gonna be our little secret. What happens in Vegas, stays in Vegas.)
